AES
AES, or Advanced Encryption Standard, is a widely used symmetric encryption
algorithm that ensures secure data transmission and storage.
Employing a key for both encryption and decryption, AES
operates on fixed-size blocks of data. It's recognized for its
robust security and is applied in various applications,
safeguarding sensitive information globally.

DES
DES (Data Encryption Standard) is a widely used symmetric key algorithm for
encrypting electronic data. It employs a 56-bit key to transform
plaintext into ciphertext, providing confidentiality.
Developed by IBM in the 1970s, DES has been widely adopted for
secure communication and data protection, although
it's now considered insecure due to its short key length.

MONOALPHABETIC
Monoalphabetic refers to a simple encryption technique where each letter
in the plaintext is consistently replaced with a corresponding
letter in the ciphertext, maintaining a one-to-one mapping
throughout. This method involves a fixed substitution pattern,
making it vulnerable to frequency analysis and easy to
decipher with sufficient data.

POLYALPHABETIC
Polyalphabetic refers to a cryptographic method where multiple
cipher alphabets are used during encryption, enhancing security by
complicating pattern analysis. In simpler terms, polyalphabetic
encryption involves employing various alphabetic substitutions
in a systematic manner to create a more complex and secure cipher.

COLUMNAR
Columnar refers to a format where data is organized in vertical
columns, with each column representing a specific attribute or
variable. It involves storing and processing information in a
column-wise fashion, facilitating efficient data retrieval and
analysis. It streamlines data management by storing similar
types of data together, enhancing performance.
